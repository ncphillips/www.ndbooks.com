Happy Poetry Month: ‘Two American Scenes’, by Lydia Davis & Eliot Weinberger

  • Published
  • April 9th, 2013
Eliot Weinberger
Lydia Davis

This week’s Poetry Pamphlet excerpt(s) comes from Two American Scenes, a collaborative work by Eliot Weinberger and Lydia Davis. The pamhplet, in the words of editor Jeffrey Yang, consists of "two different scenes of nineteenth-century Americana, lyrically recounted by Lydia Davis in ’Our Village’ and Eliot Weinberger in ’A Journey on the Colorado River.’ The first is set in idyllic, rural New England; the second during an expedition through the river canyons of the still wild West. Break out your butter churner and hold onto your hats!"

from "A Journey on the Colorado River"
by Eliot Weinberger

It seems a long way up to the world of sunshine.

Camped in a grove of cedars, box-elder, and dead willows. A sudden whirlwind scatters our fire and sets the site ablaze. We rush for the boats, ears scorched, hair and clothes singed, carrying what we can. The cook stumbles and falls, and away goes the mess-kit down the river. Plates are gone; spoons gone; our knives and forks are gone.

The unceasing roar of rushing water.

*   *   *

from "Our Village"
by Lydia Davis

The sea appeared perfectly smooth, a mile and a half away,
and the vessels inshore were long in passing
while those in the offing, which I thought very near
and so small that I could take them in my hands,
seemed to hang all day as white specks
between the sky and the water.
The voices, so kind,
of those who led me from place to place,
their mild words, were in exact harmony
with the picture of the slow-moving vessels
from those windows
and the vessel on the wall clock
that kept time with the pendulum,
rising and falling on a billowy sea.

Ah! Goddess! Take pity on my fever and my pain! Vol. 1 on Exercises in Style
