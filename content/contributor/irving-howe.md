Irving Howe (1920â€“1993) was an American literary and social critic. A prominent voice among the New York Intellectuals, he wrote essays and criticism for _Partisan Review_, the _Nation_, _Commentary_ and _Politics_. From its inception in 1954 until his death 1993, Howe edited the political magazine _Dissent_. In 1976 he won a National Book Award for his history of East European Jews in America, _World of our Fathers_.
